About

My objective is to develop my professional qualifications by engaging in industry-leading projects. At the same time, I seek to participate in collaborative research with industrial and academic partners worldwide. My expertise and interests lie in the fields of software quality, software interoperability, and software engineering. My research primarily focuses on software quality metrics and interoperability issues related to context alignment, data mapping, and interface definition languages.


I have strong skills in formulating and executing projects. I am a hardworking, highly self-motivated, and achievement-oriented professional. Additionally, I have a high capacity for analyzing and addressing issues, adopting strategies, and integrating new knowledge and technical skills to develop effective solutions.
I take particular pride in my ability to build collaborative networks connecting industry and academia. As evidence of this, I organized the second edition of the Software Quality Workshop in 2017, bringing together leading software companies, academic experts, and students of computer and systems engineering to share experiences and knowledge. Furthermore, I completed an eight-month research stay at the University of Bordeaux, where I achieved significant research results in collaboration with some of the most prominent experts in the field of systems interoperability.
